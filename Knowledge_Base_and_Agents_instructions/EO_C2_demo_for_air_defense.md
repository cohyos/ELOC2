# EO C2 demo for air defense

##### [**Undermind**](https://undermind.ai)

---

**Research Goal:** Find academic literature relevant to building a demo of an electro-optical command-and-control system for air defense that manages inputs from a C4ISR system with multiple radars and other sources. The system should support assigning one or more electro-optical sensors to investigate tracked objects, using multiple sensors to derive a 3D target through triangulation, and resolving multiple targets within a sensor field of view. Include both the technical foundations and more recent work, covering both autonomous sensor management and human-in-the-loop/operator-supervised control, as well as system-level architecture and demo-relevant design for showing the system over a map with simulated targets and electro-optical sensor management.

## Topic Breakdown

**EO command and control for air defense: End-to-end architecture and algorithms for cueing and managing EO/IR sensors from multi-radar C4ISR tracks, including multi-sensor 3D triangulation, multi-target resolution in EO fields of view, and demo-oriented map visualization with operator-supervised control**

- *Air-defense C2 and recognized air picture generation: multi-sensor track management and fusion architecture*
- *Radar–EO/IRST integration and cueing handoff: slew-to-cue, cooperative search and track, and fusion algorithms*
- *Sensor management and tasking for EO/IR and multi-radar systems: allocation, scheduling, and process refinement with human supervision*
- *Multi-sensor 3D localization and triangulation from EO bearings: geometry, observability, and error propagation*
- *Resolving multiple targets in EO fields of view and associating with external tracks: MHT, PMHT, TBD, and multitarget filtering*
- *Sensor registration and bias mitigation for radar–EO fusion: alignment, cross-covariance handling, and timing*
- *Demo-oriented simulation, map display, and operator workstations: geospatial visualization of tracks and sensor coverage with interactive tasking*

1\. · 1996\
**DESIGN OF A REAL-TIME MULTISENSOR SEARCH AND TRACK SYSTEM** ([link](https://doi.org/10.1111/J.1559-3584.1996.TB01573.X))\
R. Pawlak, R. Horman, R. Stapleton, and R. Headley\
*Naval Engineers Journal* · May 1, 1996 · 0 citations

> Low flying missiles present severe difficulties to shipboard radar systems, which must detect and track them in order to support defensive weapons systems. Propagation conditions at these altitudes create an impossible task for a radar only search and track system. To this end, the Naval Surface Warfare Center, Dahlgren Division (NSWCDD) has been investigating the use of a horizon search infrared (IR) sensor for assisting the radar in the target detection and tracking task. Ongoing scientific experiments have determined the characteristics of both the radar and IR sensor systems necessary to support this task. The definition of these sensors, along with knowledge of the propagation conditions have been drivers for the design of the algorithms and techniques for effectively integrating multisensor data. In the system described in this paper, active and passive sensor data is integrated to achieve improved firm track range. Detections from a horizon search infrared (IR) sensor and a horizon search radar are provided to a central sensor integration processor where both track initiation and radar dwell scheduling are performed. Significant increases in the detection range of the radar are achieved by using a high energy waveform along cued bearings generated by high confidence detections from the IR sensor. Previous results and recent real-time field test data are presented which support the system concept.

4\. · 2019 · 4.9 cit/yr\
**Heterogeneous Track-to-Track Fusion in 3-D Using IRST Sensor and Air MTI Radar** ([link](https://doi.org/10.1109/TAES.2019.2898302))\
M. Mallick, Kuo-Chu Chang, S. Arulampalam, and Yanjun Yan\
*IEEE Transactions on Aerospace and Electronic Systems* · Feb 8, 2019 · 35 citations

> Only a few publications exist at present on heterogeneous track-to-track fusion (T2TF). A common limitation of the current work on heterogeneous T2TF is that the cross covariance due to common process noise cannot be computed. This is due to the fact that two local trackers use different dynamic models, and hence, it is difficult to account for the common process noise. We consider a heterogeneous T2TF problem in three dimension (3-D) using a passive infrared search and track (IRST) sensor and an active air moving target indicator (AMTI) radar with the nearly constant velocity motion of the target. The active AMTI tracker uses the Cartesian state vector with 3-D position and velocity, and the dynamic model is linear. A passive IRST tracker commonly uses modified spherical coordinates (MSCs) for the state vector, where the dynamic model is nonlinear. In this formulation, the common process noise is explicitly modeled in both dynamic models. Therefore, it is possible to take into account the common process noise. We use the cubature Kalman filter (CKF) in both trackers due to its numerical stability and improved state estimation accuracy over existing nonlinear filters.
